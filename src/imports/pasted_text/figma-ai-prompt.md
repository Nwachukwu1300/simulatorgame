## Project Brief

Design the full UI system for "Simulator Collection," a premium single player game. The game places the player in control of a stationary object such as a coconut, a park bench, or a stone idol. Nothing moves. Nothing happens to the player. The world around the object is fully alive with weather, wildlife, and background events.

Design the interface as if this were a serious, prestige, story driven AAA release. Treat every screen with total sincerity. The comedy comes entirely from the gap between the absurd premise and the polish of the presentation. The UI must never wink at the joke, never use playful fonts, and never look like a parody or a meme.

## Art Direction

Blend four references.

From Firewatch, take the warm color grading, the flat graphic silhouettes of foreground elements against gradient skies, and the sense of a quiet, personal story.

From The Last of Us Part II, take the restrained serif typography, generous negative space, and a menu system built around a single full bleed photographic background with minimal text floating over it.

From Journey, take the use of one dominant color per scene, soft gradients, and a small central symbol as a focal point.

From Ghost of Tsushima, take the elegant thin typography with wide letter spacing, subtle brush texture accents, and wind or particle movement drifting across the frame.

The result should feel like a first party studio spent three years on this game.

## Typography

Use a refined serif or a clean high contrast sans for titles. Wide letter spacing on all caps labels. Small, quiet body text. No rounded, no playful, no display fonts. No condensed gaming fonts with sharp aggressive angles. Titles should feel literary, closer to a prestige drama title card than a video game logo.

## Color Palette

Cinematic and muted. A dark base with one warm accent color per environment, amber for the beach, deep green for the park, and burnt orange for the jungle. Use a soft vignette on every background. Avoid saturated primary colors anywhere in the UI chrome.

## Layout Principles

Favor negative space over density. Menu options sit in a single column, left aligned or centered, never more than four or five words each. UI chrome stays minimal, thin lines, no heavy panels or boxes. Backgrounds are full bleed photographic environment renders with a slight blur or depth of field behind the text.

## Screens To Design

### Main Menu

Full bleed cinematic background that slowly drifts, showing a blended glimpse of the beach, park, and jungle. Game title "Simulator Collection" centered or left aligned in refined serif type. Menu options below it, Play, Settings, Quit. Small studio credit line at the bottom corner, styled like a real publisher logo lockup.

### Settings Menu

Same cinematic treatment, background blurred further to push focus onto the panel. Two options only, Graphics with a Low and High toggle, and Mode with a Realistic and Arcade toggle. Style the toggles like restrained AAA settings sliders, not game like switches.

### Arcade Mode Warning

A single modal styled like a serious system message or an achievement popup. It tells the player they remain an inanimate object regardless of mode, then the mode reverts to Realistic automatically. Deliver this with total gravity, same type treatment as a save confirmation or a critical game alert.

### Simulator Selection Screen

Three large vertical cards, one per simulator, each with full bleed photographic art, Coconut Simulator on a tropical beach, Bench Simulator in a city park, Jungle Idol Simulator in an ancient temple. Style these like chapter select screens from a story driven game, large title, one line of moody description text, subtle hover state that brightens the card and reveals a select prompt.

### Loading Screen

Blurred environment art in the background, a thin minimal progress bar, and a rotating tip line delivered in complete seriousness, for example "Remaining still requires no additional input."

### In Simulator HUD Overlay

Extremely minimal. Bottom left or bottom right corner only. Small status lines in a quiet serif or mono font, Status Stationary, Movement None, Objective Continue Existing. Style it like a restrained diegetic HUD from a prestige survival game, thin dividing lines, no icons unless extremely subtle.

### Pause Menu

Background freezes and dims with a slight blur. Options stack vertically, Resume, Settings, Quit To Menu. Same typography and spacing rules as the main menu.

## Motion And Feel

Transitions are slow fades with slight scale changes. Hover states use a soft glow or a thin underline that expands. Nothing snaps or bounces. Every interaction should feel expensive and unhurried.

## What To Avoid

No comic fonts, no bright primary UI colors, no cartoon icons, no emoji, no mascots, no arcade era pixel fonts, no meme style callouts, no exclamation marks in copy, and nothing that visually signals this is a joke. The comedy lives entirely in the premise, never in the interface.

## Deliverables

Generate desktop frames at 1920 by 1080 for each screen listed above. Keep a consistent grid, spacing system, and type scale across all frames so the set reads as one coherent, professionally art directed game.
